DetSpace: Engineering detectable pathways for bio-based production

DetSpace allows the selection and design of biological circuits for sensing of bio-based chemicals by its metabolic conversion into an allosteric transcription factor-based detectable molecule. In that way, DetSpace provides a tool for precision engineering of screening and dynamic regulation of bio-based pathways of industrial interest for the bioeconomy.

A walk-through tutorial

Start

In this tutorial, we assume that the user wants to produce vanillin, a flavoring agent, in the E. coli chassis (for engineered production routes see for instance Yoon et al, Biotechnol. Lett., 2005.)

Chassis selection

Therefore, we start by selecting the E. coli chassis:

Target compound selection

Then, the target product vanillin is selected from the list of producible compounds:

Detectable compound selection

From the list of detectable compounds that can be linked to the vanillin product, GABA (γ-amminobutyric acid), an inhibitory neurotransmitter, is selected:

Pathway generation

Next, the detectable pathway that converts vanillin into GABA is generated and graphically shown:

If multiple pathways are available, they will appear at the table on the right panel, each one indicating the number of steps and its associated score according to the RetroRules reaction viability score. By hovering or clicking on a pathway, the selected pathway will be highlighted on the graph.

Detailed information about each compound in the pathway can be obtained by clickin on the node in the pathway, including chemical structure and link to databases. In the case of the producible compound, i.e., vanillin, it is possible to display the production pathway associated with the compounds. In the case of the detectable compound, i.e., GABA, information about the transcription factor that can be allosterically regulated by this effector is provided.

Similarly, for each enzyme in the pathway, information about the chemical transformation can be obtained by clicking on the node. In addition, a link to the Selenzyme service is provide to search for putative enzyme sequences catalyzing the desired reaction.

Exporting the results

The results can be exported into Cytoscape exchange JSON format and in Galaxy SynBioCAD rpSBML format, allowing in that way its further processing to obtain the plasmid sequences of the design of the associated genetic circuit.
